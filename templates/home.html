{% trans %}Taxi to Berlin{% endtrans %}

{% trans %}Stories of Greek migrants in the German capital{% endtrans %}
{% trans %}

Whenever the topic of Greek immigrants came up in the German discourse in the last five years, it was always about the crisis. It seems like the only reason for Greeks to come to Germany anymore is to flee the situation in their home country. Whatever happened to the idea of “Europe” and choosing freely where you want to live and work? There’s a lot more to this story. “Taxi to Berlin”

{% trans %}I. Meet Katerina{% endtrans %}
{% trans %}
I feel at home in both countries.
Katerina Michailidi

Katerina Michailidi was born in Athens in 1986. She moved to Berlin in March of 2008 upon being accepted as an Erasmus student. She studied theatre at Freie Universität.

It was my choice…

When the crisis struck her home country in late 2008, Katerina was completing her studies from a distance in Berlin. She was entering her final year of studies. She experienced the disturbing events in Greece from a distance, feeling powerless as a passive observer watching the crisis unfold.

{% trans %}II. Meet Ares{% endtrans %}
{% trans %}
I used to be Greek-German. Now I'm only Greek.
Ares Kalandides

Ares Kalandides was born in Athens in 1964. He came to Berlin in 1989, two days after the wall came down. He spent half of his life in Greece and half in Germany. The economic crisis in 2008 completely changed his life.

Freedom was the main reason I came to Berlin. I didn't plan to go back at all.
Ares Kalandides

CAPTION?

Ares moved to Berlin at a time when the city was undergoing complete transformation. His life took some interesting turns. With a degree in French literature and fluent in several other languages, Ares became involved in urban planning by chance. He started his own urban development compnay in:polis which now employs 14 people.

The 2008 crisis changed my life completely.
Ares Kalandides

Germany is a country in general and Berlin is a city in particular where people think twice before making racist remarks, before making sexist remarks.
Ares Kalandides
